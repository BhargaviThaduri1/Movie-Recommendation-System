About Recommendation


The system starts by collecting data on the movies in the dataset, including their genre, director, cast, and plot summary. This data is then preprocessed and transformed into numerical vectors using techniques such as bag-of-words and TF-IDF. These vectors are then used to calculate similarity scores between movies using cosine similarity or other distance metrics. When a user enters their movie preferences into the system, the system compares the user's preferences to the preprocessed data and calculates similarity scores between the user's preferred movies and the other movies in the dataset. The system then recommends the top-rated movies that are most similar to the user's preferences.

Why Content Based?


They can make recommendations even for items that are unpopular or niche, as long as the user's preferences match the content of those items. This is in contrast to collaborative filtering systems, which rely on the preferences of other users to make recommendations and can struggle to make accurate recommendations for items that are not widely popular.And also hey do not require any explicit ratings or feedback from users, as they can infer the user's preferences from the content of the items that they have already consumed. This can be particularly useful in cases where users are reluctant to provide ratings or feedback.
